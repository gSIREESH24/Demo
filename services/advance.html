Frequently Asked Questions

Got Questions? We've Got Answers.

What is PMS and how is it different from Mutual Funds?

In PMS, securities are held directly in your demat account and the portfolio is customized for you. Mutual funds pool money from many investors into a common fund. PMS allows for greater personalization and a more focused portfolio, typically starting at ₹50 lakhs as mandated by SEBI.

What are the different categories of AIFs?

SEBI classifies AIFs into three categories. Category I includes infrastructure, social venture, and SME funds. Category II covers private equity and real estate funds. Category III includes hedge funds and complex strategies. The minimum investment is ₹1 crore for all categories.

What are Market Linked Debentures (MLDs)?

MLDs are debt instruments where returns are linked to a market index or rate rather than a fixed coupon. They can offer capital protection along with potential upside participation in equity markets, making them useful for investors who want some downside protection with growth potential.

We then match you with curated managers from our vetted list, comparing performance track records, strategy consistency, and fee structures before making a recommendation.

Are these investments liquid? Can I exit anytime?

PMS investments are generally liquid — you can redeem within a few working days, subject to applicable exit loads. AIFs typically have a lock-in period ranging from 3 to 7 years depending on the fund. Structured products have a fixed maturity date. These details are clearly communicated before you invest.

What are the tax implications for PMS and AIF investments?

For PMS, gains are taxed at the investor level based on the holding period of individual securities — similar to direct equity taxation. AIF taxation varies by category; Category III funds are taxed at the fund level. MLDs enjoy indexation benefits under certain conditions. Our advisors will walk you through the tax implications specific to your situation.
